The Talking Garden

A one-hour, hands-on workshop where every bed is a lab and every plant a lesson.

Designed to grow curious, confident children — through science, nature, and discovery.

4 garden stations 60-minute workshop Aligned to JMG plant-part curriculum Take-home keepsake
01

The experience

The Talking Garden turns a single visit into four guided discovery stations. Children rotate through tactile, screen-and-soil activities — naming plant parts, feeding the worm farm, growing a seed, and tracing food back to the garden — then leave with a certificate they earned themselves.

Science

Children name the four plant parts and describe what each one does.

Stewardship

Children learn what composts and how worms turn scraps into healthy soil.

Food literacy

Children connect everyday foods to the plant parts they come from.

Confidence

Every child participates, succeeds, and takes home proof of their learning.

02

The four stations

Each station pairs a tablet-guided prompt with a real garden activity. Tap “See it live” to preview exactly what a child experiences on screen.

🌻
Station 1 · Botany

Meet Sunny the Sprout

Children tap each part of a plant and uncover its “garden secret,” then find the same parts on real plants in the beds.

Objective: name & explain roots, stem, leaves, flower
Materials: tablet · sample plants
🪱
Station 2 · Composting

The Worm Café

Children sort food scraps and trash into “feed the worms” or “no thanks,” connecting directly to the garden’s worm-farm program.

Objective: distinguish compostable vs. non-compostable
Materials: tablet · real scraps · worm bin
🌱
Station 3 · Life cycles

Seed to Sprout

Children give a seed sun and water and watch it advance through each growth stage, learning what plants need to thrive.

Objective: sequence the plant life cycle
Materials: tablet · seeds · cups & soil
🥕
Station 4 · Food literacy

Which Part Do We Eat?

Children match familiar foods to the plant part they come from — carrots are roots, broccoli is a flower — tying nutrition back to the garden.

Objective: link foods to plant parts
Materials: tablet · sample produce
03

The hour, minute by minute

A simple rotation any facilitator can run. Children move in small groups; the tablet guides each station while hands stay in the soil.

0 – 10 min

Welcome to the garden

Meet Sunny and set the question: what is a plant made of, and how does it grow?

10 – 40 min

Station rotations

Small groups cycle through the four stations — tablet prompt, then the matching hands-on garden task.

40 – 52 min

Garden walk

Children find the real roots, leaves, flowers, and compost they just explored on screen.

52 – 60 min

Junior Garden Scientist badge

Each child is named, celebrated, and sent home with a printed certificate.

04

Grounded in real curriculum

The content isn’t improvised — it maps to established early-childhood garden education, so the learning is credible and age-appropriate.

JMG plant-part themes

Built on the Junior Master Gardener / Texas A&M AgriLife early-childhood framework of roots, stems, leaves, and flowers.

STEAM through agriculture

Science, observation, and food systems delivered the way Dew of Heaven already teaches — in the garden, by doing.

Food & soil literacy

Composting and farm-to-plate concepts that extend the garden’s existing worm-farm and self-sufficiency programs.
